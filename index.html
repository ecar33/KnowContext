Method (2.11) SWOT Analysis

Discovering context about how modern universities in the United States are structured

Strengths (Internal)

  • Clear hierarchical governance (boards, presidents, provosts, deans) establishes accountability.
  • Standardized academic units (schools, departments, programs) support scale and consistency.
  • Accreditation systems provide quality assurance and legitimacy.
  • Dedicated offices for admissions, advising, aid, and student services support the full student lifecycle.

Weaknesses (Internal)

  • Highly siloed academic and administrative units create fragmented user experiences.
  • Shared governance and committee processes slow decision-making and change.
  • Complex policies and procedures increase cognitive load for students and faculty.
  • Legacy and overlapping IT systems result in inconsistent interfaces and workflows.

Opportunities (External)

  • Growth of online, hybrid, and flexible programs enables new structural models.
  • Improved data collection and analytics can increase transparency and accountability.
  • Industry and community partnerships create clearer pathways from education to employment.
  • System modernization efforts create opportunities to redesign processes around user needs.

Threats (External)

  • Projected enrollment declines place financial pressure on institutions.
  • Political and regulatory changes may reduce institutional autonomy.
  • Rising skepticism about cost and return on investment challenges traditional models.
  • Alternative credential providers compete on speed, clarity, and flexibility.
